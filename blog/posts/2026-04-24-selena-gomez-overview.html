Selena Gomez Biography: Singer, Actress, Producer

Published 2026-04-24 · by Content Review

Selena Gomez is an American singer, actress, and producer who built one of the most enduring multi-platform careers of her generation. She rose to fame on Disney Channel's Wizards of Waverly Place, then became a chart-topping recording artist and a producer of award-winning television. Today she is also a beauty-industry founder and a vocal mental-health advocate.

Early life and career

Born July 22, 1992, in Grand Prairie, Texas, she was raised by her mother, Mandy Teefey, who worked multiple jobs while taking her to auditions. Her first credit came at age seven on Barney & Friends. The Wizards of Waverly Place audition in 2006 was the career hinge — the show ran four seasons, won an Emmy, and made the young actress a household name across millions of family living rooms.

Music and acting career

She moved into music with the band Selena Gomez & the Scene, then went solo with Stars Dance in 2013. Acting credits widened beyond Disney with Spring Breakers, the Hotel Transylvania franchise, and the Hulu hit Only Murders in the Building. In 2008 she founded the production company July Moon, which produced Netflix's 13 Reasons Why.

Philanthropy and mental health advocacy

A 2014 lupus diagnosis and a 2017 kidney transplant became part of how the singer talks publicly about chronic illness. She has been a UNICEF Goodwill Ambassador since 2009, and in 2020 she launched Rare Beauty, whose Rare Impact Fund directs a percentage of revenue to mental-health resources for young people. Her accomplishments now span pop music, prestige TV, and a values-driven beauty brand.
